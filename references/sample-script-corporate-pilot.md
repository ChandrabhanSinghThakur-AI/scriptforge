# Sample Script — Corporate Pilot Cold Open

## CORPORATE - Episode 1 "First Blood"

FADE IN:

INT. LIFT LOBBY - CORPORATE TOWER - MORNING

A gleaming corporate tower. Glass everywhere. The kind that says "we raised Series C."

The elevator DINGS. Doors open.

RAHUL SINGH (34) steps out. Italian shoes, tailored navy suit, a watch that costs more than the receptionist's annual salary. Eyes scan the floor like a predator entering new territory.

He carries no bag. Just a slim folder — black leather. The contents will ruin three people's careers today.

RECEPTIONIST
(startled)
Good morning, sir. You must be—

RAHUL
(already walking past)
Finance wing. Kaun sa floor?

RECEPTIONIST
Sir, actually aapka orientation—

RAHUL
(stops, turns, smiles — cold)
Orientation meri zaroorat nahi. Mujhe numbers chahiye. Orientation unhe do jinhe rakhna hai.

He walks. The receptionist exchanges a look with the security guard. Both swallow.

INT. OPEN PLAN OFFICE - STARTUP FLOOR - CONTINUOUS

CHAOS. The quintessential Indian startup: bean bags nobody sits on, a foosball table gathering dust, whiteboards covered in dead sprint plans.

DEVELOPERS hunch over screens. Someone's playing lo-fi hip-hop from a speaker. Empty Maggi cups. Red Bull cans.

Rahul stands at the entrance. Takes it ALL in. His expression doesn't change.

RAHUL (V.O.)
Har failing startup ka ek signature smell hota hai. Stale coffee, desperation, aur false optimism ka cocktail. Inhone Series B raise ki hai, lekin Series B ka matlab hota hai — ab galtiyaan expensive hongi.

He walks through. Heads turn. Conversations die.

DEVELOPER 1
(whispering)
Yeh kaun hai?

DEVELOPER 2
New CFO. Suna hai McKinsey se aaya hai.

DEVELOPER 1
Shit.

DEVELOPER 2
Exactly.

Rahul reaches the glass cabin at the end. The nameplate still reads: "PRIYA MEHTA - CFO"

He peels it off. Pockets it. Enters.

INT. CFO CABIN - CONTINUOUS

Rahul sets his folder on the desk. Opens it. Inside: a single printed sheet.

CLOSE ON: A list of names. Seven names. Red marks next to five.

He pulls out his phone. Dials.

RAHUL
(into phone)
HR head ka number bhej. Aaj shaam tak panchch log nikalenge. Unke laptops lock karo abhi.
(beat)
Haan, abhi. Kal nahi. Abhi.

He hangs up. Leans back. Looks out through the glass at the oblivious floor.

RAHUL (V.O.)
Log sochte hain CFO ka kaam numbers hai. Nahi. CFO ka kaam hai — decide karna ki kaun ki salary company afford kar sakti hai. Aur kaun ki nahi.

He smiles. It doesn't reach his eyes.

SMASH CUT TO:

TITLE CARD: CORPORATE

FADE OUT.

---

## NOTES ON THIS SCENE:

Structure used:
- Cold open (no context, immediate intrigue)
- Character intro via ACTION not exposition
- V.O. reveals internal world
- Hinglish dialogue establishes milieu
- Ends on power moment + title card

Techniques:
- "Show don't tell" — we KNOW Rahul is dangerous from behavior, not dialogue about him
- Supporting characters react to him (showing his effect on others)
- Specific details (Italian shoes, Maggi cups, foosball table) ground the world
- The folder = visual mystery (what's in it?) that pays off
- Cold open ends on a PROMISE: these people's lives are about to change
